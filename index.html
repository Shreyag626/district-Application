One Observation, Honest

A thing I'd fix

"The Dining Carnival cashback is prominent — but the redemption path isn't."
What I noticed: The hero banner on the District home screen does a great job grabbing attention with "Get assured 10% cashback." But when I tried to trace the full journey — where does the cashback land, how do I claim it, how do I know it worked — it required more taps than the promise warranted. Many times I got those buffet offers, which I never move forward with
What I'd explore: A lightweight "cashback journey indicator" — something as simple as a persistent pill after dining that says "₹X is on its way" — could reduce support queries, increase trust, and improve repeat booking rates. The cost is low. The signal it sends about the product's reliability is high. Little long duration for cashback expiry rather than just 7 days

🧠 This is the kind of thing I notice as a user — and the kind of thing I'd want to spend my days solving as a generalist. Ambiguity isn't a blocker for me. It's usually where the interesting work is.
